# Project 2: Technical Description / Explainer

## Overview

In this assignment, you will write a technical explanation of a complex concept for a non-expert audience.

Unlike the Instructional Guide, which focuses on *how to do something*, this project focuses on **how something works or why it matters**. Your goal is to make a complicated idea clear, accurate, and engaging without oversimplifying it.

This type of writing is common in fields like software development, healthcare, engineering, and science communication.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will:

* Explain complex ideas in clear, accessible language
* Adapt technical content for a non-expert audience
* Organize information logically using sections and hierarchy
* Use examples, analogies, and visuals to improve understanding
* Strengthen clarity, precision, and readability

## Assignment Description

Choose a technical concept, system, or process related to your field of study (or an area you want to explore).

Your task is to explain:

* What it is
* How it works
* Why it matters

Your explanation should be written for a specific non-expert audience, such as:

* First-year college students
* General public readers
* Your parents

## Possible Topics

You may choose your own topic, or consider examples like:

* How machine learning works
* What APIs are and how they function
* How blockchain technology works
* What happens during photosynthesis
* How GPS systems determine location
* What cybersecurity encryption does

All topics must be approved before you begin drafting.

## Required Components

### 1. Document (Markdown File)

Your explanation should include:

* **Title**

* **Introduction**

  * Define the concept
  * Identify the intended audience
  * Explain why the topic matters

* **Main Sections**

  * Clear breakdown of the concept into parts
  * Logical organization (use headings and subheadings)
  * Definitions of key terms

* **Explanation Strategies**

  * At least one example
  * At least one analogy or comparison

* **Conclusion**

  * Summarize key ideas
  * Reinforce importance or real-world relevance

### 2. Visual Element

Include at least one visual, such as:

* Diagram
* Chart
* Annotated image

The visual must:

* Directly support understanding
* Be referenced and explained in the text

### 3. GitHub Submission

* Upload your document to your GitHub repository
* Maintain clean organization and naming
* Submit to Canvas via repository link

## Audience Requirement

You must clearly define your audience and write at an appropriate level.

Ask yourself:

* What does my reader already know?
* What might confuse them?
* How can I make this easier to understand?

Avoid writing as if your reader is an expert.

## Evaluation Criteria

Your work will be evaluated based on:

* **Clarity of Explanation** – Is the concept understandable?
* **Organization & Structure** – Logical, easy-to-follow sections
* **Audience Awareness** – Appropriate level and tone
* **Use of Examples & Analogies** – Helps clarify complexity
* **Visual Effectiveness** – Supports understanding
* **Style & Mechanics** – Grammar, readability, precision
* **Technical Accuracy** – Correct explanation of the concept

## Tips for Success

* Imagine explaining this to someone outside your field
* Break complex ideas into smaller parts
* Define terms before using them
* Use analogies carefully (make sure they actually help)

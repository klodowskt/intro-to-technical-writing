# Project 3: Process Documentation **OR** Usability Report

## Overview

In this assignment, you will choose **one of two professional technical writing genres**:

* **Option A: Process Documentation** – Explain how a workflow or system operates
* **Option B: Usability Report** – Evaluate how easy a tool or website is to use

Both options focus on real-world workplace writing, including clarity, organization, and revision based on user needs.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will:

* Analyze how people interact with tools, systems, or workflows
* Write structured, professional documentation
* Apply audience awareness to real-world contexts
* Use evidence (observation, testing, or explanation) to support your writing
* Practice revision and collaborative feedback

## Choose One Option

## **Option A: Process Documentation**

### Description

Write a document that explains **how a process works from start to finish**.

This is commonly used in workplaces to:

* Train employees
* Standardize workflow
* Document internal systems

Your goal is to make the process **clear, complete, and easy to follow**.

### What to Document

Choose a process that:

* Has multiple steps or stages
* Involves decisions, tools, or roles
* Is relevant to your field or interests

Examples:

* Software development workflow
* Hiring or onboarding process
* Content publishing workflow
* Data analysis pipeline

### Required Components

Your document should include:

* **Title & Overview**

  * What the process is
  * Who uses it
  * Why it matters

* **Process Stages**

  * Organized into clear steps or phases
  * Use headings and/or numbered sections

* **Detailed Explanation**

  * What happens at each stage
  * Tools or roles involved
  * Key decisions or variations

* **Visual Element**

  * Flowchart, diagram, or structured visual

* **Conclusion**

  * Summary or key takeaways

## **Option B: Usability Report**

### Description

Evaluate the usability of a **website, app, or digital tool** by observing real users.

Your goal is to identify:

* What works well
* What causes confusion
* How the design could be improved

### What to Test

Choose a tool that:

* Has a clear purpose and interface
* Can be tested by other users
* Is appropriate for analysis

Examples:

* A website (shopping, news, education)
* A mobile app
* A software interface

### Required Components

Your report should include:

* **Introduction**

  * What you tested
  * Purpose of the evaluation

* **Methodology**

  * Who your users are (2–3 participants recommended)
  * What tasks they completed
  * How you collected data (notes, screen recording, etc.)

* **Findings**

  * Specific usability issues or successes
  * Observations from user behavior
  * Patterns across users

* **Recommendations**

  * Specific, actionable improvements
  * Clear connection to observed problems

* **Visual Evidence (Optional but encouraged)**

  * Screenshots or annotated examples

## Shared Requirements (Both Options)

### 1. Document (Markdown File)

* Clearly structured with headings and sections
* Written in a professional, concise style
* Uses formatting to improve readability

### 2. GitHub Submission

* Upload to your repository
* Maintain clean organization
* Submit to Canvas via link

### 3. Audience Awareness

Your document should be written for a specific audience, such as:

* Employees
* Managers
* Developers
* Designers

## Process & Deadlines

* Either option should be between 700-800 words
* **Option Selection + Proposal:** (due Week 10)
* **Draft for Peer Review:** (due Week 11)
* **Final Submission:** (due Week 12)

## Evaluation Criteria

Your work will be evaluated based on:

* **Clarity & Organization** – Logical structure and readability
* **Depth & Detail** – Completeness of explanation or analysis
* **Audience Awareness** – Appropriate tone and level
* **Use of Evidence** – Explanation (Process) or observations (Usability)
* **Style & Mechanics** – Grammar, clarity, conciseness

## Tips for Success

### If you choose Process Documentation:

* Think beyond the "steps" into **systems and flow**
* Be thorough: don’t skip “obvious” steps
* Use visuals to show relationships

### If you choose Usability Report:

* Focus on **user behavior**, not personal opinion
* Be specific: what exactly caused confusion?
* Make recommendations practical and actionable

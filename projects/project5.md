# Project 5: Final Portfolio + Reflection

## Overview

In this final project, you will revise your work and publish a professional portfolio website showcasing your technical writing efforts throughout the semester.

This portfolio will serve as a public-facing representation of your skills, suitable for sharing with employers, instructors, or peers.

You will also write a reflection statement analyzing your growth as a writer and communicator over the course of the semester.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will:

* Revise and improve previous writing based on feedback
* Present your work in a polished, professional format
* Demonstrate control of organization, clarity, and style
* Reflect on your development as a writer
* Build a portfolio you can use beyond this course

## Assignment Description

You will:

1. Review your major projects and revise 2 of them
2. Publish them on a GitHub Pages portfolio site
3. Write a reflection on your learning and growth

Your final portfolio should highlight your **best work**, not just completed work.

## Required Components

### 1. Portfolio Website (GitHub Pages)

Your site must include:

* **Home Page**

  * Brief introduction (who you are, what you study, interests)
  * A picture is optional

* **Projects Section**

  * Project 1: Instructional Guide
  * Project 2: Technical Description
  * Project 3: Process Doc *or* Usability Report

* **Project Pages**

  Each project should:

  * Be revised and polished
  * Include a short description or context
  * Be clearly formatted and readable

### 2. Revision Requirement

You must **substantively revise at least 2 major projects**.

Revisions may include:

* Improving clarity and organization
* Adding or improving visuals
* Refining structure or formatting
* Addressing feedback from peers or instructor

### 3. Reflection Statement (500–800 words)

Your reflection should address:

* What you learned about technical writing this semester
* How your writing changed over time
* How this portfolio represents your skills
* Challenges you faced and how you addressed them
* Your strengths as a writer
* Areas you still want to improve

### 4. Submission

* Your portfolio must be published using **GitHub Pages**
* All files should be organized and accessible
* Submit a link to your live site
* Reflections should be submitted as Google Docs

## Process & Deadlines

* **Portfolio Draft:** (due Week 16)
* **Final Submission:** (due Week 16)

## Evaluation Criteria

Your work will be evaluated based on:

* **Quality of Revisions** – Meaningful improvement of past work
* **Portfolio Organization & Design** – Clarity, navigation, professionalism
* **Content Quality** – Strength of included projects
* **Reflection Depth** – Thoughtfulness and specificity
* **Audience Awareness** – Professional tone and presentation
* **Technical Execution** – Functional GitHub Pages site

## Tips for Success

* Don’t just fix surface errors—revise for clarity and impact
* Choose your strongest work to highlight
* Keep your design simple and readable
* Test your site (links, formatting, navigation)
* Be honest and specific in your reflection

## Final Note

This portfolio is something you can continue to build and use beyond this course. Your writing and presentation should reflect professional standards.
